Overview

Dr. Su is a distinguished specialist in molecular biology and bioinformatics, with extensive experience in the gut microbiome and its relationship to human diseases. He excels in analyzing large multi-omics datasets for both clinical and research applications.

Dr. Su received his Ph.D. in Molecular Biology from [University Name] and completed postdoctoral training in Bioinformatics at [Institution Name]. His research focuses on the interactions between the gut microbiome and various health conditions, utilizing advanced computational techniques to derive insights from complex biological data.

As first or corresponding author, he has authored over fifty original research papers in high-impact journals, such as Nature Microbiology, Cell Host & Microbe, Lancet Infectious Diseases, Nature Communications, and Gut. Recognized for his significant contributions, Dr. Su has secured several competitive grants and numerous awards, including a gold medal from the International Exhibition of Inventions Geneva and the Young Investigator Award from KDDW. Additionally, he serves on the editorial board of iMeta and reviews for peer-reviewed journals including Nature Methods, Advanced Science, and Gut.
